This repository contains resources, notes, and materials for the **Foundations of Cybersecurity** course, which is the first course in the **Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate** on Coursera. This course provides a comprehensive introduction to the core concepts and practices of cybersecurity, designed for beginners to build a solid foundation in the field.

## Overview

The **Foundations of Cybersecurity** course is the first step in the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate program. It introduces learners to the field of cybersecurity, covering essential concepts, terminology, and practical skills needed for entry-level roles such as Security Analyst.

## Course Structure

The course follows the Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate curriculum, divided into the following modules:

1. **Introduction to Cybersecurity**
2. **The Cybersecurity Landscape**
3. **Core Security Concepts**
4. **Security Roles and Responsibilities**
5. **Threats, Vulnerabilities, and Exploits**
6. **Security Controls and Frameworks**
7. **Security Compliance and Regulations**
8. **Security Operations and Monitoring**

## Key Topics

### Introduction to Cybersecurity
- What is cybersecurity and its importance in modern organizations
- Cybersecurity job roles and career paths (Security Analyst, Security Engineer, CISO, etc.)
- Cybersecurity domains and specializations

### The Cybersecurity Landscape
- Current threat landscape and emerging trends
- Threat actors and their motivations (cybercriminals, nation-states, hacktivists, insiders)
- Common attack types (malware, phishing, DDoS, SQL injection, etc.)

### Core Security Concepts
- The CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability)
- Defense in Depth strategy
- Principle of Least Privilege
- Risk management fundamentals

### Security Roles and Responsibilities
- Cybersecurity team structures
- Role of Security Analysts in organizations
- Cross-functional collaboration with IT, legal, and business teams
- Professional ethics and standards

### Threats, Vulnerabilities, and Exploits
- Types of vulnerabilities (software, configuration, human, physical)
- Vulnerability management processes
- Exploitation techniques and attack vectors
- Common vulnerabilities and exposures (CVEs)

### Security Controls and Frameworks
- Security controls: preventive, detective, corrective
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover)
- NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)
- Other industry frameworks (ISO 27001, COBIT, ITIL)

### Security Compliance and Regulations
- Regulatory requirements (GDPR, HIPAA, PCI DSS, CCPA)
- Compliance processes and audits
- Data protection and privacy laws
- Industry-specific security standards

### Security Operations and Monitoring
- Security operations center (SOC) functions
- Security information and event management (SIEM)
- Incident detection and response basics
- Security monitoring and alerting

# Manage Security Risks - Summary Notes (Google Cybersecurity Professional Certificate)

## Module 1: Introduction to Risk Management

### What is Risk Management?
Risk management is the process of identifying, assessing, and prioritizing risks, followed by the coordinated and economical application of resources to minimize, monitor, and control the probability or impact of unfortunate events. In cybersecurity, risk management focuses on protecting organizational assets from threats.

### Importance of Risk Management
- Helps organizations make informed decisions about security investments
- Reduces the likelihood of security incidents
- Minimizes the impact of security breaches
- Ensures compliance with regulations
- Protects organizational reputation and customer trust

### Risk Management Frameworks
- **NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF)**: A structured process for managing security risks
- **ISO 31000**: International standard for risk management
- **COBIT**: Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies
- **FAIR**: Factor Analysis of Information Risk

### Key Risk Management Terminology
- **Risk**: The potential for loss, damage, or destruction of an asset
- **Threat**: A potential cause of harm to an asset
- **Vulnerability**: A weakness that could be exploited by a threat
- **Asset**: Something of value to an organization
- **Impact**: The effect of a risk event on an asset
- **Likelihood**: The probability of a risk event occurring

## Module 2: Risk Assessment Processes

### Risk Assessment Methodologies
- **Qualitative Risk Assessment**: Subjective assessment based on expert judgment
- **Quantitative Risk Assessment**: Objective assessment using numerical values
- **Semi-quantitative Risk Assessment**: Combines qualitative and quantitative methods

### Asset Identification and Valuation
- Identifying and cataloging organizational assets
- Assigning value to assets based on importance to the organization
- Classifying assets by sensitivity and criticality
- Understanding interdependencies between assets

### Threat Assessment and Classification
- Identifying potential threats to assets
- Classifying threats by type (cyber, physical, human)
- Assessing threat likelihood and capability
- Prioritizing threats based on impact

### Vulnerability Assessment and Prioritization
- Identifying vulnerabilities in systems and processes
- Assessing vulnerability severity using CVSS scores
- Prioritizing vulnerabilities for remediation
- Understanding vulnerability exploitability

### Risk Calculation and Scoring
- **Risk Score = Likelihood Ã— Impact**
- Using risk matrices to categorize risks (low, medium, high, critical)
- Prioritizing risks based on their scores
- Communicating risk scores to stakeholders

## Module 3: Threat Modeling

### Threat Modeling Concepts
- Identifying potential threats to systems and applications
- Understanding threat actor capabilities and motivations
- Mapping threats to system components
- Identifying potential attack vectors

### Common Threat Modeling Frameworks
- **STRIDE**: Spoofing, Tampering, Repudiation, Information Disclosure, Denial of Service, Elevation of Privilege
- **PASTA**: Process for Attack Simulation and Threat Analysis
- **CVSS**: Common Vulnerability Scoring System (v3.1)
- **DREAD**: Damage, Reproducibility, Exploitability, Affected Users, Discoverability

### Threat Mitigation Strategies
- **Preventive Controls**: Stop threats from occurring
- **Detective Controls**: Identify threats that have occurred
- **Corrective Controls**: Respond to and mitigate threats
- **Compensating Controls**: Provide alternative protection

## Module 4: Vulnerability Management

### Vulnerability Management Processes
1. **Discovery**: Identifying assets and vulnerabilities
2. **Assessment**: Evaluating vulnerability severity
3. **Prioritization**: Determining which vulnerabilities to fix first
4. **Remediation**: Applying patches or fixes
5. **Verification**: Ensuring vulnerabilities are resolved
6. **Reporting**: Communicating vulnerability status

### Vulnerability Scanning and Assessment
- Using vulnerability scanning tools (e.g., Nmap, Nessus)
- Understanding scan results
- Validating vulnerabilities
- False positive identification and handling

### CVSS (Common Vulnerability Scoring System)
- **Base Score**: Intrinsic characteristics of a vulnerability
- **Temporal Score**: Current characteristics of a vulnerability
- **Environmental Score**: Context-specific characteristics
- **Overall Score**: Combined score (0-10, with 10 being most severe)

### Remediation Planning and Execution
- Creating remediation plans
- Assigning responsibilities
- Setting timelines for fixes
- Monitoring remediation progress
- Documenting remediation activities

## Module 5: Risk Treatment Strategies

### Risk Treatment Options
- **Risk Mitigation**: Reducing the likelihood or impact of a risk
- **Risk Transfer**: Shifting risk to another party (e.g., insurance)
- **Risk Acceptance**: Acknowledging and accepting a risk
- **Risk Avoidance**: Eliminating the risk by stopping the activity
- **Risk Sharing**: Distributing risk among multiple parties

### Risk Mitigation Techniques
- Implementing security controls
- Hardening systems and applications
- Training employees on security awareness
- Regularly updating software and systems
- Implementing access controls

### Risk Acceptance Criteria
- Determining when to accept a risk
- Documenting risk acceptance decisions
- Monitoring accepted risks
- Reassessing risks on a regular basis

### Risk Communication
- Communicating risks to stakeholders
- Tailoring communication to audience
- Documenting risk management activities
- Reporting risk status to management

3. **Risk treatment options vary**: Choose the right strategy based on risk severity and organizational context
4. **Security controls are essential**: Implementing effective security controls reduces risk

6. **Continuous improvement is necessary**: Regularly review and update risk management processes
7. **Compliance is important**: Ensure risk management practices comply with regulations
8. **Employee awareness is vital**: Training employees on security best practices reduces human risks
